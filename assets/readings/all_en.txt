English story

This week I’m very busy, but on the weekend I’m free. We decide to go to the city together. Tomorrow morning we walk from home to the station, then to the subway. “How far is it from here to the mall?” my friend says, “In about a moment, we’ll arrive.”

After we arrive at the mall, there are many people—everyone is busy. We look at these clothes, pants, and shoes. That lady says, “This pair of shoes is expensive; that pair is cheap.” We choose the cheap pair. Then I want to buy a gift. There are quite a few customers, and the attendant is busy, but still says, “Please.”
I ask, “Will this gift work?” The attendant replies, “Of course.”
I say, “Please give me a receipt.”

Later we go to a restaurant. We look at the menu and choose hot pot; we also want beef and vegetables. Tonight it’s hot, so we drink juice. After eating, I want to give the attendant money, but I can’t find my wallet. Just now I put the wallet on the table; later I forgot. What should we do? I’m nervous and worried.

We hurry back to the restaurant. The attendant says, “The wallet is over there.” We go there: the wallet is on the table, and the phone is there too. I say, “Okay!” For safety, I put the wallet in our bag, then put the phone in our bag. On the weekend we go to the park to play—this time, we’re all happy.
